# Italy Honeymoon — Revised Full Itinerary (v2)
**Palmi → Lucca · The Romantic Full Itinerary**
Fly out Friday · Land Saturday · Home the second Sunday — **15 nights in Italy**

You only drive the scenic stretches. At Salerno you drop Car #1 and arrive on the Amalfi Coast **by ferry**; trains link Salerno–Rome, Rome–Orvieto–Chiusi, and Florence–Lucca.

**Changes from v1:** Maratea is now a long lunch stop (not an overnight), giving the Amalfi Coast a 4th night; Herculaneum replaces Pompeii (as an easy luggage-free half-day from the coast); added Siena, San Miniato al Monte vespers, the Aventine Hill in Rome, an Orvieto lunch stop, the Vitaleta chapel sunrise, an Amalfi lemon-terrace tour, and a Puccini recital in Lucca; wine days restructured so no one drives after tastings; one farm dinner at the agriturismo; Cinque Terre day trip cut (Capri saved for the anniversary trip); Pisa's Piazza dei Miracoli added as a departure-day bonus.

---

## Trip Overview

A south-to-north honeymoon linking both families' ancestral cities — **Palmi** (his, Calabria) and **Lucca** (hers, Tuscany) — with the Amalfi Coast, Rome, the Val d'Orcia countryside, and Florence in between. Built for romance: slow mornings, sunset terraces, vineyard lunches, and light-touch cities rather than a monument checklist.

**Flights:** book one open-jaw / multi-city ticket — San Antonio → Lamezia Terme (SUF), returning Pisa (PSA) → San Antonio. No nonstops; expect 1–2 connections via a European hub. Roughly $900–1,200 per person in shoulder season (more in summer). Price Houston (IAH) as an alternative origin — often more options.

Coast, countryside, and the two family towns hold **11 of the 15 nights**; big cities get a light, romantic touch. Only **6 hotel check-ins** across 15 nights — no one-night stands with your luggage.

### When to go

**Late May (top pick):** warm and swimmable (sea ~66–70°F), long golden evenings, coast fully alive; busier and a bit pricier than April. **Mid-April:** greenest countryside, wildflowers and wisteria, lightest crowds, lowest prices — but the sea is too cold to swim. **Avoid** early March (much of the coast still closed) and July–August (heat, crowds, peak prices). If traveling in April, confirm coastal hotels, the Salerno–Amalfi ferries, and boat operators are running, and book around Easter and the Apr 25 / May 1 holidays.

## Day-by-Day

**Fri — Fly out**
Overnight flight San Antonio → (European hub) → Lamezia Terme.

**Sat (night 1) — Arrive Calabria · his ancestral city**
Land at Lamezia, pick up Car #1, drive ~1 hr to Palmi/Tropea. Slow first evening in his family's town: Piazza Primo Maggio, sunset from the Monte Sant'Elia belvedere, first aperitivo.

**Sun (night 2) — Scilla & Tropea**
Lunch in Scilla's Chianalea fishermen's quarter, houses rising from the water. Afternoon in Tropea: clifftop old town, Santa Maria dell'Isola, a long seafood sunset. (Late May: swim; April: admire.)

**Mon (night 3) — North to the Amalfi Coast, via Maratea**
Leave Tropea mid-morning. Coastal drive ~2.5 hrs to **Maratea** for a long lunch: the little port, a wander through the hilltop lanes, the Christ the Redeemer statue viewpoint. Then ~2 hrs on the autostrada to **Salerno**; drop Car #1 by late afternoon and take the ferry to your Atrani/Amalfi base — arriving by sea, not traffic. **Know the last ferry time before you set out** (typically early evening, weather-dependent); backup is the SITA bus or a taxi. Terrace dinner as the light fades.

**Tue (night 4) — Ravello**
Up to Villa Cimbrone's Terrace of Infinity and the Villa Rufolo gardens. Lazy lunch. Stay up in Ravello into the evening — after the day-trippers leave it's the most romantic place on the entire coast. **Splurge sunset dinner in Ravello**; check whether a Villa Rufolo chamber concert falls on your night.

**Wed (night 5) — The unscheduled day**
Yours. Options, not obligations — all car-free from your base:

- **Herculaneum half-day (~5–6 hrs door to door):** morning ferry to Salerno (~35 min), regional train to Portici-Ercolano (~45 min), short walk/cab to the site. ~90 min–2 hrs covers it — intact second stories, wooden furniture, mosaics in place. Back on the coast by mid-afternoon with the evening intact.
- **Pompeii full day (~7–8 hrs):** ferry to Salerno, regional train to Pompei station (~25–30 min), which puts you at the quieter **Piazza Anfiteatro entrance** (skip the mobbed Porta Marina gate). Timed ticket + a guide for 2.5–3 hrs (unguided Pompeii is just a lot of hot rubble). Late lunch, then a return ferry gliding into Amalfi around golden hour. Vast and exposed — hats, water, early start, especially in the May window. If choosing this, move the lemon-terrace tour to Thursday morning and keep Wednesday evening unbooked: no reservation, just the terrace.
- **Pick one, not both.** Stacking Herculaneum + Pompeii on the same train line is possible but it's ruins fatigue by 2pm — not honeymoon material.
- **Or none:** the **lemon-terrace walk and limoncello tasting** above Amalfi town · beach and spa · nothing at all on your terrace. (April alternative: the free Path of the Gods hike.)

**Thu (night 6) — On the sea**
A private or small-group sunset boat: swim coves, the Furore fjord, a cold bottle as the cliffs turn gold.

**Fri (night 7) — To Rome**
Ferry/bus to Salerno, fast train to Rome (~2 hrs). Lantern-lit Trastevere dinner.

**Sat (night 8) — Romantic Rome**
No marathon: Villa Borghese gardens and a rowboat on the lake, the Pantheon, gelato. Late afternoon on the **Aventine Hill** — the Orange Garden at sunset with its sweep over the Tiber to St. Peter's, and the famous keyhole at the Knights of Malta gate next door. The Trevi after dark, rooftop nightcap. One big sight only if you're keen (pre-booked).

**Sun (night 9) — To the Val d'Orcia, via Orvieto**
Train Rome → **Orvieto** (~1 hr 15): funicular up the rock, the cathedral façade, a long lunch. Onward train to **Chiusi** (~40 min), pick up Car #2, into the hills to your agriturismo. Sunset over the vines; dinner in Montepulciano or at the farm.

**Mon (night 10) — Slow Tuscany**
Pienza (pecorino + the perfect piazza), a thermal soak at Bagno Vignoni, a long vineyard lunch. Golden hour at the **Cappella della Madonna di Vitaleta** and the San Quirico cypress groups. **Tonight: the farm dinner at your agriturismo** — under the pergola, with nowhere to drive afterward.

**Tue (night 11) — Vines & downtime**
Optional sunrise hot-air balloon over the cypress hills — or the free version: a DIY **sunrise drive to the Vitaleta chapel** with cappuccinos in hand, the best light of the whole trip. Wine day, designed so no one drives after tasting: either walk between the historic cellars **inside Montepulciano** (De' Ricci, Contucci and company are in the town itself), or **hire a driver for a Montalcino/Brunello day**, or book a tasting at the agriturismo. The pool, with nowhere to be.

**Wed (night 12) — To Florence, via Siena**
Drive north and give **Siena 3–4 hours**: the Piazza del Campo, the striped Duomo interior (it outdoes Florence's), lunch. Park outside the walls (Santa Caterina escalators / Il Campo lots) — never enter the ZTL. Continue ~1 hr 15 to Florence Airport, drop Car #2 (outside the ZTL by design), tram into town. Oltrarno dinner.

**Thu (night 13) — Florence, unhurried**
One museum at your own pace (Uffizi or Accademia, pre-booked), the Oltrarno artisan lanes, a leisurely lunch. Early evening: **vespers with Gregorian chant at San Miniato al Monte** (typically ~5:30–6:30pm — confirm the schedule), then the ten-minute walk down to **Piazzale Michelangelo** for sunset over the whole city. Rooftop nightcap.

**Fri (night 14) — To Lucca · her ancestral city**
Short train to Lucca (~1 hr 20). Bike the Renaissance walls at golden hour.

**Sat (night 15) — Lucca**
Her family's city: Torre Guinigi, a picnic on the walls, the comune/parish for family history. Evening: the **Puccini e la sua Lucca recital at San Giovanni** (nightly, usually ~7pm) — Puccini sung a few streets from where he was born — then dinner in the oval Piazza dell'Anfiteatro.

**Sun — Fly home**
Train to Pisa (~30 min). If the flight allows, an hour in the **Piazza dei Miracoli** (Leaning Tower, Duomo, Baptistery) en route to the airport. Depart PSA → San Antonio.

## Romantic Experiences

Private or sunset boat on the Amalfi Coast · Villa Cimbrone gardens (~€10) · sunset dinner in Ravello after the crowds leave (+ Villa Rufolo concert if the calendar aligns) · lemon-terrace walk & limoncello tasting above Amalfi · rowboat in Villa Borghese · the Aventine Orange Garden at sunset + the Knights of Malta keyhole · couples' Tuscan cooking class · vineyard lunch and walkable cellar tastings (Vino Nobile in town; Brunello with a driver) · farm dinner at the agriturismo · Bagno Vignoni thermal soak · sunrise at the Vitaleta chapel (DIY, free) or the hot-air balloon (splurge) · golden-hour honeymoon photo shoot (Florence or Val d'Orcia — the Vitaleta light is the best of the trip) · Gregorian-chant vespers at San Miniato al Monte + Piazzale Michelangelo sunset · biking Lucca's walls at golden hour · a Puccini recital in her ancestral city.

**Extras that fit without adding nights:** Herculaneum with a guide on the free coast day · a vintage Fiat 500 or Vespa vineyard tour · a couples' spa afternoon · an hour at Pisa's Piazza dei Miracoli on the way to the airport.

**Deliberately cut:** the Cinque Terre day trip from Lucca (2+ hrs each way, heaving crowds, and it would steal half of her family city's two days — the emotional finale). **Capri is saved for the anniversary trip** — with only one unscheduled coast day, it's better protected than spent.

## Practical Notes

- **International Driving Permit** (AAA, ~$20) before you go — required alongside your license in Italy.
- **ZTL zones:** never drive into a town-center restricted zone (cameras, automatic fines). Both car drop-offs (Salerno, Florence airport) are outside them by design. In **Siena**, park at the Santa Caterina escalators or Il Campo lots outside the walls.
- **The Monday transfer is the one long day:** Tropea → Maratea lunch (~2.5 hr coastal) → Salerno (~2 hr autostrada) → ferry. Leave mid-morning, keep the Maratea stop to 2–3 hours, and know the last ferry time (early evening, weather-dependent; SITA bus or taxi as backup).
- **Vesuvius ruins — do them from the coast, not the car-drop day:** both sites are luggage-free by ferry + regional train on the free Wednesday. Herculaneum = half-day, calmer, better preserved; Pompeii = full day, iconic, needs a guide and the Piazza Anfiteatro entrance. Pick one.
- **Wine without driving:** Vino Nobile cellars are walkable inside Montepulciano; for Brunello/Montalcino, hire a driver or book the tasting at your agriturismo. Rural Val d'Orcia roads are unlit gravel — never tastings-then-driving.
- **Orvieto stopover ticketing:** book Rome→Orvieto and Orvieto→Chiusi as separate legs; the funicular from Orvieto station to the old town runs every ~10 min.
- **Trains:** Frecciarossa/Italo Salerno–Rome in advance on the Trenitalia or Italo app for cheaper reserved seats; regional legs are fixed-price and flexible.
- **Rent the smallest manual-transmission car** (automatics cost markedly more in Italy).
- **Reserve early:** flights, the Atrani (4-night) and Florence rooms, the balloon and boat, the Ravello dinner, the Montalcino driver, Uffizi/Accademia or Colosseum/Vatican slots, the Puccini recital, and the headline dinners.
- **Family history:** at both Palmi and Lucca, the Ufficio Anagrafe (registry office) and parish archives hold birth/marriage records — a short email ahead in Italian can tee up a visit.
- **San Miniato vespers** times shift seasonally — check the basilica's schedule during your final week.
